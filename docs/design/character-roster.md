# Character Roster

> Status: **Core four locked** (Phase 0). Full ensemble target is 6-8 —
> remaining slots will be filled by wielders the group meets through the
> story (per the "drawn together by the awakening event" structure). Combat
> kits (attribute type, Keepsake form, Resonant Echo) are deferred to Phase 1
> once the power-system numbers are on paper — this doc captures *identity*.

## Suzu — The Protagonist

- **Age**: ~17-21 (player character)
- **Voice**: Mostly silent — personality comes through small reactive beats
  (a glance, a short line, a choice) rather than full dialogue. Built for
  player projection without being a blank slate.
- **Core trait**: Quiet and gentle on the surface, with hidden resolve
  underneath — the "soft exterior, steel interior" duality the name itself
  reflects (*Suzu* = "bell": a small, quiet sound that nonetheless carries and
  resonates outward — a name that quietly mirrors the game's own Strata
  Resonance identity).
- **Story role**: Her sudden Resonance awakening is the inciting incident —
  the same moment that crystallizes her first Resonant Echo from whatever
  formative memory triggers it. Everything else in the early game radiates
  out from that beat.
- **Player-customizable**: Suzu *is* the player's avatar — her role, name
  (working default), and story beats stay as designed, but her attribute type
  isn't authored here, and isn't picked from a menu either — it's *revealed*
  through how the player actually responds in her awakening scene (see
  [[setting-and-factions]] for the locked shape of that moment). The reaction
  and the reveal are the same beat, matching the lore's own rule that "a
  character's type isn't an arbitrary assignment, it's a reflection of their
  inner emotional shape." This is what "built for player projection without
  being a blank slate" means in practice: the identity is templated, the
  specifics are yours.

## Raphael — "The Livewire" (Friend A)

- **Age**: ~17-21, pre-existing friend (no powers... yet)
- **Core trait**: Energetic, talkative, a little chaotic — brings the
  comic-relief/levity energy that balances the hidden world's heavier stakes.
- **Story role**: When the hidden world reveals itself, he's the one who
  leans *in* — thrilled rather than scared. His eventual awakening comes
  easily, almost joyfully: a living answer to "what if this power were a gift,
  not a burden?" — one half of the emotional mirror that plays off Suzu's
  unspoken feelings about her own power.
- **Attribute — Surge**: "Volatile, charged, sudden — passion, anger, thrill,
  urgency" (see [[power-system]]) maps directly onto "The Livewire" — energetic,
  a little chaotic, leans into intensity rather than away from it.

## Wren — "The Anchor" (Friend B)

- **Age**: ~17-21, pre-existing friend (no powers... yet)
- **Core trait**: Level-headed, a little guarded — the one who keeps the group
  tethered to who they were before everything changed.
- **Story role**: Reacts to the hidden world with hesitation, maybe outright
  resistance. Their eventual awakening is a *struggle* — reluctant, possibly
  something they try to suppress — raising the harder question Suzu's silence
  can't answer for her: *what if you don't want this?* The other half of the
  emotional mirror.
- **Attribute — Bedrock**: "Dense, solid, immovable — resolve, conviction,
  stubbornness, protectiveness" (see [[power-system]]) reflects the friend who
  keeps the group tethered and resists change rather than drifting with it —
  and pairs neatly with the "Anchor" nickname (grounding, immovable) without
  the conduit-item collision now that the conduit is named "Keepsake."

## Salvatore — The Mentor

- **Age**: ~37-41 (about 20 years older than the core cast)
- **Connection**: Personal, not institutional or faction-assigned — he knew
  someone close to Suzu (a parent, guardian, or older sibling) from his own
  time in the hidden world, and made a quiet promise to look out for her. His
  guidance comes from "I told them I'd watch over their kid," not an
  organization's orders.
- **Name note**: *Salvatore* means "protector/savior" — a meaning that quietly
  mirrors his role the same way *Suzu*'s does, without either name spelling it
  out on the surface.
- **Attribute — "Harmonic" (unique, off-wheel)**: Where the standard five
  types each represent a single emotional register, Harmonic represents
  *emotional mastery* — resonating in tune with all of them at once. This is
  what makes him **effective against and resistant to every standard type**
  (see [[power-system]] for the full breakdown), framed as something earned
  through age and experience rather than a birthright. It's also the
  mechanical reason he's *able* to mentor wielders of completely different
  types — he genuinely understands every emotional register, not just one.
- **Story role**: Early-game guide into the hidden world for Suzu; his
  personal stake in her wellbeing (rather than a faction's agenda) is what
  gives the relationship its emotional weight — and a possible source of
  tension later if his past in the hidden world resurfaces. **As the story
  grows, his role expands beyond Suzu** — he becomes a mentor figure to other
  cast members too (a natural extension of Harmonic's "understands every
  register" identity), reinforcing the found-family shape of the group rather
  than keeping the mentor bond exclusive to one person.

## How the group comes together

Drawn together by the awakening event itself — not assigned roles, not a
pre-built organization. Suzu's sudden, unregistered awakening (and the
attention it draws) pulls Salvatore toward her out of personal obligation,
while Raphael and Wren — already her friends before any of this started —
get pulled along simply by being close to her when it happens. The found
family forms organically out of proximity and circumstance, the same way the
Keepsake forms organically out of a wielder's awakening (see
[[power-system]]) — one more thread tying the cast design back to the world's
core mechanics.
